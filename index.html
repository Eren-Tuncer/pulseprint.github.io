The electrocardiogram (ECG) is as a powerful biometric modality due to its indiviually unique patterns, making it ideal for secure authentication systems. However, recent advances in AI have raised concerns about the generation of synthetic ECG data, which could potentially compromise the integrity and security of ECG-based biometric systems.

This project aims to develop an AI model capable of distinguishing between authentic and AI-generated ECG biometric data. By training the model on both authentic and synthetic ECG signals, the system will learn to identify subtle differences, ensuring the security and reliability of ECG-based biometric authentication.

We believe this project will solve the issue of AI-generated ECG data being used to compromise identity and access control systems. The end goal is to create a robust, scalable platform that can be used for secure identification across various applications, such as healthcare, financial transactions, and personal identification systems.
